About

I am Fudong Wang (王福东), Assistant Professor in the Math. Department at Great Bay University.

Background

I earned my Ph.D. in Mathematics from the University of South Florida. My dissertation is titled Long-time Asymptotics for Integrable PDEs in the AKNS Hierarchy in the Presence of Poles by the dbar Steepest Descent Method, advised by Professor Wen-Xiu Ma.

Research Interests

  • Spectral theory of nonlinear integrable PDEs, especially focusing NLS and KdV type equations.
  • Soliton and breather gases: average densities, fluxes, and thermodynamic limits of finite-gap solutions.
  • Riemann-Hilbert and dbar steepest descent methods for long-time asymptotic analysis.
  • Applications of complex analysis in free-boundary and related mathematical physics problems.
