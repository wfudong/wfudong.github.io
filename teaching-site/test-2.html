Test 2: Geometry in R³

Estimated time: 40–50 minutes

Focus: Practice the core skills from Lecture 2: quadric recognition, traces, parametrized curves/surfaces, tangent objects, and arc length in $\mathbb{R}^3$.

What You'll Practice

  • Classifying quadric surfaces from equations
  • Finding and interpreting traces and level curves
  • Parametrizing curves and surfaces
  • Computing tangent vectors, tangent lines, and tangent planes
  • Calculating arc length for parametrized space curves

  1. Exercise 1: Classify a Quadric

    Classify $x^2 + y^2 - z^2 = 1$.

    Hint

    Two plus signs and one minus sign with right side 1 usually gives a hyperboloid.

    Solution

    It is a hyperboloid of one sheet.

  2. Exercise 2: Horizontal Trace

    For $z = x^2 + y^2$, the trace at $z=9$ is a circle. Enter its radius.

    Hint

    Set $z=9$ so $x^2+y^2=9$.

    Solution

    Radius is $3$.

  3. Exercise 3: Parametrize an Intersection Curve

    The cylinder $x^2+y^2=4$ intersects the plane $z=x+1$. If $x=2\cos t$ and $y=2\sin t$, which $z(t)$ is correct?

    Hint

    Substitute $x=2\cos t$ into $z=x+1$.

    Solution

    $z(t)=2\cos t+1$.

  4. Exercise 4: Tangent Plane (Graph Form)

    For $z=x^2+y^2$ at $(1,2,5)$, enter the tangent plane coefficients for $z=ax+by+c$.

    Hint

    $f_x=2x$, $f_y=2y$ at $(1,2)$.

    Solution

    $z=2x+4y-5$.

  5. Exercise 5: Tangent Line Direction

    For $\mathbf r(t)=\langle t,t^2,t^3\rangle$, enter a direction vector of the tangent line at $t=1$.

    Hint

    Differentiate first, then evaluate at $t=1$.

    Solution

    $\mathbf r'(t)=\langle 1,2t,3t^2\rangle$, so at $t=1$ direction is $\langle 1,2,3\rangle$.

  6. Exercise 6: Arc Length in 3D

    For $\mathbf r(t)=\langle 3\cos t,3\sin t,4t\rangle$, $0\le t\le2\pi$, choose the arc length.

    Hint

    Speed is constant: $\|\mathbf r'(t)\|=5$.

    Solution

    $L=\int_0^{2\pi}5\,dt=10\pi$.

  7. Exercise 7: Tangent Plane (Implicit Form)

    For the sphere $x^2+y^2+z^2=14$ at $(1,2,3)$, choose the tangent plane.

    Hint

    Use gradient normal $\nabla F=(2x,2y,2z)$.

    Solution

    Plane: $x+2y+3z=14$.

  8. Exercise 8: Recognize the Saddle Surface

    Classify $z=x^2-y^2$.

    Hint

    Opposite signs in $x^2$ and $y^2$ produce a saddle.

    Solution

    Hyperbolic paraboloid.

  9. Exercise 9: Surface Parametrization

    Choose a correct parametrization of the sphere $x^2+y^2+z^2=4$.

    Hint

    Use spherical-angle coordinates.

    Solution

    Option (a) is correct.

  10. Exercise 10: Helix Tangent Angle

    For $\mathbf r(t)=\langle R\cos t,R\sin t,bt\rangle$ ($R,b>0$), choose $\cos\theta$ where $\theta$ is the angle between the tangent and the $z$-axis.

    Hint

    Take unit tangent and dot with $\mathbf k$.

    Solution

    $\cos\theta=\dfrac{b}{\sqrt{R^2+b^2}}$, constant in $t$.
