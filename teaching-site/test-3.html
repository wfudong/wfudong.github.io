Practice 3: Limits, Continuity, and Partial Derivatives

Estimated time: 45–60 minutes

Focus: Based on Lecture 3: multivariable limits, continuity, partial derivatives, differentiability, and chain rule.

What You'll Practice

  • Path-based limit analysis
  • Continuity at the origin for piecewise functions
  • Computing first-order partial derivatives
  • Linearization and tangent plane formulas
  • Chain rule with one parameter

  1. Exercise 1: Path Test

    For $f(x,y)=\dfrac{xy}{x^2+y^2}$, compare limits along $y=x$ and $y=-x$. Does the full limit at $(0,0)$ exist?

  2. Exercise 2: Positive Limit Example

    $\lim_{(x,y)\to(0,0)}\dfrac{x^2y^2}{x^2+y^2}=$ ?

  3. Exercise 3: Continuity at Origin

    $f(x,y)=\begin{cases}\dfrac{x^2y}{x^2+y^2},&(x,y)\neq(0,0)\\0,&(0,0)\end{cases}$ is continuous at $(0,0)$?

  4. Exercise 4: Partial Derivatives

    For $f(x,y)=x^2y+\sin(xy)$, compute $(f_x,f_y)$ at $(1,0)$.

  5. Exercise 5: Linearization

    For $f(x,y)=x^2e^y$ at $(1,0)$, choose the linearization $L(x,y)$.

  6. Exercise 6: Tangent Plane

    For $z=x^2+y^2$ at $(1,2,5)$, enter $(a,b,c)$ in $z=ax+by+c$.

  8. Exercise 8: Differentiability Fact

    Is the statement true: “If both partial derivatives exist at a point, then the function is differentiable there.”
