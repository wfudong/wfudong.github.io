Submission Guidelines

Practice: Completed online in this portal. No submission required—your score is recorded automatically.

Questions? Email me with the subject line Calculus II: [Your Question] or request office hours via the LMS message center. I aim to respond within 24 hours on weekdays.

Academic Integrity

Homework must reflect your own thinking. You may discuss ideas with classmates, but write solutions independently. Direct copying from peers, online solutions, or generative AI is a violation. When in doubt, cite your source or ask me.

Practice exercises and exams must be completed without external resources. Suspected violations will be reported to the Dean of Students.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I submit homework?

Submit all homework as a PDF file or clean handwritten scan via the course LMS. Filenames should follow the format: YourName_HW#.pdf (e.g., JohnSmith_HW1.pdf). Include your student ID and submission date on your cover page.

What if I submit homework late?

Late submissions are accepted up to 3 days after the due date, with a 10% penalty per day. Submissions more than 3 days late will not be accepted. If you anticipate a late submission due to illness or emergency, email me in advance.

How are practice exercises scored?

Practice exercises are auto-scored when you submit your answers. Your best score across up to 3 attempts counts toward your grade. You can retry immediately after reviewing hints and solutions. No time limit applies to practice attempts.

What is the attendance policy?

Regular attendance is expected. Lectures provide crucial discussions, examples, and live problem-solving. If you must miss a class, let me know in advance via email. Recorded lectures (if available) are posted within 48 hours on the LMS.

Can I use a calculator or computer during exams?

For the midterm and final exam, computational aids (calculators, computers, phones) are not permitted. However, a basic scientific calculator may be allowed—I'll clarify this one week before each exam. Tests in this portal require no external tools.

What if I don't understand a concept?

Start with the lecture notes and hints in the tests. If you're still stuck, request office hours via email with the subject line Calculus II: [Concept] and a brief description of what's confusing. I aim to respond within 24 hours on weekdays.

How do I contact you with questions?

Email: Use the LMS message center or email directly with the subject Calculus II: [Topic]. Office Hours: Request by email or through the LMS message center—include 2–3 preferred time slots. Expected response time: Within 24 hours on weekdays.
