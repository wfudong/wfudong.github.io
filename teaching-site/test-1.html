Test 1: Vectors Warm-up

Estimated time: 30–45 minutes

Focus: Work through this interactive checklist to revisit the main ideas from Lecture 1: lines and planes, dot and cross products, determinants, and matrices acting on vectors.

What You'll Practice

  • Scalar triple product and coplanarity
  • Matrix multiplication and linear transformations
  • Dot products, magnitudes, and projections
  • Cross products and area calculations
  • Determinants and volume scaling

  1. Exercise 1: Coplanarity via Triple Products

    Consider the lines

    • L1: \( \mathbf{r} = (1,-1,3) + t(2,1,-1) \)
    • L2: \( \mathbf{r} = (3,2,-1) + s(1,-2,4) \)

    Use the scalar triple product \( [\mathbf{p}_2 - \mathbf{p}_1, \mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{v}_2] \) to decide whether they lie in the same plane.

    Hint

    Compute \( \mathbf{p}_2 - \mathbf{p}_1 \) first, then form the determinant with the two direction vectors. A zero triple product means the three vectors are coplanar.

    Solution

    \( \mathbf{p}_2 - \mathbf{p}_1 = (2,3,-4) \), \( \mathbf{v}_1 = (2,1,-1) \), \( \mathbf{v}_2 = (1,-2,4) \). The triple product equals \(-3 \neq 0\), so the lines are skew and have no common plane.

  2. Exercise 2: Matrix Image of a Vector

    The shear matrix \( A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \) acts on \( \mathbf{x} = (-1, 3) \). Compute \( A\mathbf{x} \).

    Hint

    Think column-wise: \( A(x, y) = x(1,0) + y(2,1) \). Multiply \( x \) by the first column and \( y \) by the second column.

    Solution

    \( A\mathbf{x} = (-1 + 2\cdot 3, 3) = (5, 3) \). The shear keeps \( y \) unchanged and shifts \( x \) by twice the height.

  3. Exercise 3: Determinant and Orientation

    For \( S = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{pmatrix} \), record \( |\det S| \) and the orientation change.

    Hint

    For a diagonal matrix, \( \det S \) is the product of the diagonal entries. The sign tells you whether the transformation flips orientation.

    Solution

    \( \det S = 2\cdot(-1) = -2 \), so \( |\det S| = 2 \) and the negative sign indicates an orientation flip while areas scale by 2.

  4. Exercise 4: Composition Order Matters

    \( A = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \) stretches horizontally, \( B = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \) rotates \(90^\circ\) counterclockwise, and \( \mathbf{x} = (1, 1) \). Choose the correct statement about \( BA\mathbf{x} \) versus \( AB\mathbf{x} \).

    Hint

    Remember that matrix products apply the rightmost matrix first. Try computing \( A\mathbf{x} \) and \( B\mathbf{x} \) separately before stacking the actions.

    Solution

    \( BA\mathbf{x} = B(2,1) = (-1, 2) \) while \( AB\mathbf{x} = A(-1,1) = (-2, 1) \). The outputs differ, so the stretch and rotation do not commute.

  5. Exercise 5: Dot Product Classification

    Let \( \mathbf{u} = (2,-1,2) \) and \( \mathbf{v} = (1,1,0) \). Compute \( \mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v} \) and decide whether the angle between them is acute, right, or obtuse.

    Hint

    Multiply matching components and sum: \( \mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v} = u_1v_1 + u_2v_2 + u_3v_3 \). A positive dot product means \( \cos \theta > 0 \).

    Solution

    \( \mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v} = 2\cdot1 + (-1)\cdot1 + 2\cdot0 = 1 > 0 \), so the vectors form an acute angle.

  6. Exercise 6: Projection Components

    Project \( \mathbf{u} = (4, 1) \) onto \( \mathbf{v} = (1, 2) \). Enter the x- and y-components of \( \operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf{v}}(\mathbf{u}) \).

    Hint

    Use \( \operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf{v}}(\mathbf{u}) = \dfrac{\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v}}{\mathbf{v}\cdot\mathbf{v}}\, \mathbf{v} \). Compute the scalar coefficient first, then multiply \( \mathbf{v} \).

    Solution

    \( \mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v} = 6 \) and \( \mathbf{v} \cdot \mathbf{v} = 5 \), so \( \operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf{v}}(\mathbf{u}) = (6/5)(1, 2) = (6/5, 12/5) \).

  7. Exercise 7: Cross Product Coordinates

    Find \( \mathbf{u} \times \mathbf{v} \) for \( \mathbf{u} = (1, 2, 0) \) and \( \mathbf{v} = (3, 1, 4) \). Enter each component.

    Hint

    Set up the determinant with \( \mathbf{i}, \mathbf{j}, \mathbf{k} \) in the first row, \( \mathbf{u} \) in the second, \( \mathbf{v} \) in the third. Remember the alternating signs.

    Solution

    \( \mathbf{u} \times \mathbf{v} = (8, -4, -5) \). Its magnitude \( \sqrt{105} \) equals the area of the parallelogram spanned by \( \mathbf{u} \) and \( \mathbf{v} \).
