Problem 1: Coplanarity Check

Consider the two lines given in parametric form:

  • Line L1: r = (1, -1, 3) + t (2, 1, -1)
  • Line L2: r = (3, 2, -1) + s (1, -2, 4)

Use the scalar triple product [p2 - p1, v1, v2] (where p1 and p2 are points on the two lines and v1, v2 are their direction vectors) to decide whether the lines are coplanar.

  1. Compute the vector that connects the point on $L_1$ to the point on $L_2$.
  2. Evaluate the scalar triple product of that connector with the two direction vectors.
  3. Use your result to decide if the lines lie on the same plane.

Need a hint?

Remember that three vectors are coplanar if and only if their scalar triple product is zero. Build the connecting vector p2 - p1 and then expand the determinant.

Show solution

Taking p1 = (1, -1, 3) and p2 = (3, 2, -1) gives p2 - p1 = (2, 3, -4). The direction vectors are v1 = (2, 1, -1) and v2 = (1, -2, 4). Their scalar triple product is -3, which is non-zero, so the lines are skew and do not share a plane.
